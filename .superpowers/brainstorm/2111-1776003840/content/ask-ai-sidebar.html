How it works

1

New Chat button

Top of sidebar, always visible. Clears the chat area and starts a fresh conversation. Same pattern as Claude/ChatGPT — muscle memory works.

2

Nav items (compact row)

Compressed into a horizontal icon+label row to save vertical space. Still 1-click to Dashboard/Upload/Notes.

3

Recent Chats (scrollable)

Shows chat title (auto-generated from first message) + timestamp. Active chat is highlighted. Scrollable — shows last ~10 chats, with a "View all" link if needed. NOT a dropdown — always visible in the sidebar.

4

Your PDFs (context selector)

The active PDF gets a cyan glow — the ambient AI signal meaning "this is what the AI is reading." Click another PDF to switch context mid-conversation.

Design note: Why NOT a dropdown?

Dropdowns hide information behind a click. For chat history, students often scan titles visually — "which chat had the integration stuff?" Having them always visible in the sidebar means zero clicks to find a past conversation. This is how Claude, ChatGPT, and Gemini all do it — because it works.
